# Probation Management Procedure

## Purpose of Probation Period
The purpose of probation is to establish whether the new recruit’s performance is of an acceptable standard. It is intended for newly appointed employees only and serves to identify performance problems before permanent employment is confirmed and to reasonably address these problems in a systematic way.

## Length of Probation
The probation period should be reasonable and will depend on the nature of the job, which in turn will determine how long it will take to establish whether the recruit is performing satisfactorily or not. 

At Umuzi, the probation period is typically between three and six months, depending on the seniority or complexity of the role. The probation period will be stated in the employee’s contract of employment.

The probationary period may be extended, within reason, where the manager is not convinced that the person is performing to the required standard. If the period is extended, it should once again be done in writing. The purpose of the extension is to give the employee an opportunity to improve on the shortcomings identified.

## Poor Performance Procedure (Managing Probation)
During the probationary period, the performance of the employee should be assessed. If the employee fails to meet the required standards or displays behaviour that is incompatible with the ethos of the workplace, the employee should be advised of these shortcomings. 

This means that the manager must evaluate an employee during the probationary period and should provide regular feedback to him or her. The employee must be given reasonable evaluation, instruction, training, guidance, or counselling as required in order for the person to render satisfactory work. Where performance problems continue despite such intervention, the employer may follow incapacity procedures.

### WEEK ONE
1. **Workplace Induction**  
   The recruit must successfully be integrated into Umuzi to become a productive and confident member of the team. 

2. **Manage Goals and Expectations**  
   Ideally on the person’s first day, the manager must have a ‘kickoff’ meeting with the employee and give the new recruit a copy of his/her job description. The purpose of the meeting is to talk in detail about the duties and responsibilities and to set clear expectations around the role.  
   The manager must make sure that the information is clear and up to date, and that the employee understands the requirements and expectations.

### WEEK TWO AND FORTNIGHTLY AFTER THAT
3. **Schedule Regular One-on-One Meetings**  
   After the ‘kickoff’ meeting, the manager must schedule regular one-on-one meetings to track the new starter's progress. It is recommended that these take place at least once every two weeks and the meetings must be formal, structured and recorded in writing.  
   As well as structured one-on-ones, the manager can also schedule more informal sessions to give and receive feedback to the recruit. It is essential that issues are addressed promptly, as and when they arise, preferably face to face.

4. **Structure of One-on-One Meetings**  
   - Ask the employee how things are going and how they feel they are progressing.
   - Highlight the strengths in the person’s work performance.
   - Describe/specify the issues of concern or shortfalls.
   - Describe the expectation of how tasks should be performed, and standards required.
   - Describe the effect of performance concerns on Company operations, work team, etc.
   - Explain the consequences if employee is unable to achieve performance standards, i.e. employment concluding at end of probation.
   - Give the employee an opportunity to respond and explain his/her viewpoint of the situation and challenges he/she may be having with the work.
   - Ask the employee for ideas to help improve or correct the situation.
   - Come up with a joint solution/action plan and set targets. Determine if any further training, mentoring, or coaching is required.
   - Schedule further follow-up sessions to discuss progress.

### MONTH THREE, FOUR OR SIX (LAST MONTH OF PROBATION PERIOD)
5. **Outcome of Probation**  
   If all goes well and the manager is satisfied with their team member's performance at the end of the probation period, the successful completion of probation will be communicated to the person in writing.  
   If the manager is not satisfied with the employee’s performance, however, an extension of the probation period could be considered. This should only be done in exceptional circumstances, if the manager believes that the employee can make the necessary improvements within this extra time.  
   In most cases where the employee did not perform satisfactorily during probation, the manager should proceed to a probation review in the form of an Incapacity Enquiry.

## Probationary Review and Dismissal
Dismissal during or at the end of the probationary period will be warranted where the employee does not perform to the expected level and should be preceded by an Incapacity Enquiry where the employee must be given an opportunity to state a case in response.  
Should it become necessary to dismiss an employee during the probation period for misconduct, the normal Disciplinary Procedure will be followed.
