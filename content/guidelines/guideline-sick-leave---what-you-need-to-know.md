# Guideline: Sick Leave - What You Need to Know

Sick leave works differently from annual leave; it’s specifically for when you’re genuinely too unwell to work. Here’s the essentials 👇

## Your Sick Leave Entitlement

- You’re entitled to the number of days you’d normally work in 6 weeks (that’s about 30 days over a 3-year period, on full pay).
- Sick leave can’t be carried over, and there’s no payout for unused sick leave.

## If You’re Feeling Unwell

- Let your manager know before 08h30.
- Submit a sick leave form on the day or when you return to work.

## When a Doctor’s Note Is Required

You’ll need a doctor’s note if:

1. You’re off 2 or more consecutive days,
2. You’re off more than twice in an 8-week period, or
3. Your sick day falls next to a weekend or public holiday.

> Umuzi may verify the authenticity of medical certificates if needed.

## Doctor’s Appointments

Routine appointments (e.g. check-ups, dentist, etc.) should usually be taken as annual leave, unless your doctor officially books you off.

> Bottom line: If you’re sick, please take the time to rest and recover — that’s exactly what this leave is for 💛
