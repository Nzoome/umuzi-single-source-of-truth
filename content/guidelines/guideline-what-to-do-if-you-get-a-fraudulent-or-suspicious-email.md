# Guideline: What to Do If You Get a Fraudulent or Suspicious Email

Scam and phishing emails are getting sneaky and sometimes they look really legit. Every now and then, one might land in your inbox pretending to be from someone at Umuzi, your bank, or a well-known brand. It’s important that everyone at Umuzi knows what to look out for and how to respond safely.

## Spotting a Suspicious Email

Phishing is when someone tries to trick you into sharing personal or sensitive information (like passwords or financial details) or getting you to click on harmful links. Before you do anything, take a quick look for these red flags:

- The sender’s email looks slightly off (like umuzz.org instead of umuzi.org)
- The name and surname of the sender might be familiar, but **ALWAYS** look at the email address
- The message feels urgent - “click now or your account will be locked!”
- The tone or formatting feels weird or off-brand
- There’s a random attachment or unexpected link
- It asks you to confirm personal info, bank details, or passwords

> If your gut says “hmm, this feels dodgy”, it probably is.

## If You Get a Suspicious or Fraudulent Email

1. Do not click any links or open attachments. These can have malware linked.
2. Do not reply to the sender or forward the email to anyone else.
3. **Report the Email as Phishing or Spam in Gmail**
   - Open the suspicious message in Gmail.
   - Click the "More" icon (three vertical dots, usually next to the "Reply" arrow or button).
   - Select "Report phishing" or "Report spam"

   What this does: Reporting it helps Google's abuse prevention systems flag similar messages globally and, importantly, triggers an alert in the Google Workspace Admin Alert Center for your IT/Security team.

4. Take a screenshot that shows the full sender email address and the message without clicking on anything.
5. Share this SCREENSHOT with hloni.letuka@umuzi.org (drop a ping on WhatsApp or Slack too).
6. Delete it from your inbox and your trash folder after reporting.

## If You Already Clicked or Shared Info

> Don't panic!

1. Change your password immediately.
   - Use a strong password that's different from your previous one.
2. Report it to Hloni Letuka and Sara Brakeman via Slack right away.
   - The faster they know, the easier it is to protect your account.
3. Keep an eye out for follow-up weirdness (like password reset emails or login alerts).

## Stay Scam-Smart

Here are a few habits that go a long way:

- Always check who the email is really from - this you can see by looking at the full email address.
- Hover (don’t click) on links to see where they lead.
- Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
- Keep your computer and browser updated.
- Trust your gut — if it feels off, it probably is.
- Do not share authentication codes with anyone.
